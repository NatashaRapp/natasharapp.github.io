Introduction

When I entered the Master of Development Practice Program (MDP) program at the University of Arizona, I had few expectations. I looked forward to working on meaningful projects, learning new concepts and skills, and meeting new people across multiple cultures and backgrounds. Despite the turbulence and varied support within the MDP program, these hopes have been fulfilled. During my time as a MDP student I have had the privilege of working with diverse researchers on intersectional projects, learned a wealth of new skills, and have made connections with bright scholars from across the world.

The following pages are mere traces of the research and projects I have contributed to since my enrollment in MDP. These are pieces of a varied process, checkered with shortfalls and triumphs. Although I am proud of the work I have done and knowledge gained throughout the MDP program, what is captured in this portfolio cannot convey all of my research contributions, experience, trials and tribulations. Unillustrated in this portfolio is juggling 2-3 jobs while supporting a full-time academic schedule. Not only this, but the following pages only conveys a glimmer of the friendships and comradery that had been formed by fellow MDP peers and other members of the University of Arizona community.

The contents you are about to review are not just pieces of an assignment. Rather, the following portfolio is a visual and verbal mosaic of my journey since 2021. Although my enrollment in MDP started in 2021, my curiosity and frustration of the social and environmental systems that influence our globalized world had been percolating for many years leading up to my enrollment in MDP. The following pieces are mere tiles that made up the mosaic of my MDP journey experience. Thank you in advance for your viewership.

Program Focus and Goals

Witnessing inequities across race, class, and geography, inspired Natasha to pursue a Master’s of Development Practice degree at the University of Arizona. Through this program Natasha studied key aspects of sustainable and resilient international development. Natasha’s focus throughout the program was monitoring and evaluation techniques for scaling climate change adaptation and mitigation in a socially just manner. By engaging with research and topics such as economic anthropology, gender equity, public health and environmental policy, Natasha has developed a qualitative analysis perspective to better inform the technical skills developed during and prior to MDP. Cultivating knowledge of and implementing holistic methods realized the goals of practicing and implementing holistic frameworks address climate change’s impact on natural and human-built environments and systems.

By focusing on experiential learning, Natasha was able to achieve her goals to contribute to policy research, scientific communications, and geospatial and data visualization. Pursuing and accomplishing coursework, internships, and various employment opportunities, Natasha further developed technical skills in geographic information systems (GIS), remote sensing and satellite data analysis, and an array of digital and scientific communications. Climate Change: Impacts to Arizona Public Health

Much of the content I generated for my role with RHPP was for the benefit of program enrollees and the program instructors. In this position I created lecture materials, gathered course education materials and assignments pertaining to topics that intersected with Climate Change, Public Health, and policy. . For my portfolio, I decided to adapt some of the information I had been exposed to and create an infographic that could be used as an awareness campaign. The video infographic delivers cited information that interconnects Climate Change, environmental health, and the well-being of Arizonans. The infographic serves accurate and impactful facts in an engaging format of saturated imagery and music. Climate Change in Arizona: Local Impacts & Challenges

Climate Change in Arizona: Local Impacts & Challenges Interactive Infographic:

This infographic was created when I could not find a visually appealing map of rural Arizona while creating learning materials for the UArizona’s RHPP program. As many of the materials were cultivated for the specific benefit of the program’s enrollees, I was inspired to create an interactive infographic that would convey geographic, public health, and environmental justice information. The vector map was designed for the viewer to interact with the Arizona landscape while learning about current health and justice issues that are exasperated by Climate Change. The purpose of this static infographic is to contain a large amount of information within a succinct design. The pop-up facts were gathered through my own independent research of scholarly and journalistic-news publications. The initial vector map graphic was crafted through Adobe Illustrator.
